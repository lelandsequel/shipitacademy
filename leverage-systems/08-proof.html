Module 08: Proof

Leverage Systems Weekend — Sunday, 1:00 PM

Objective

Capture proof that your leverage system works: before/after comparison, time saved, and output quality assessment. Proof is what makes your positioning credible and your outreach effective.

Duration

90 minutes

Facilitator Guide

Frame (10 min)

"Anyone can claim they work faster with AI. Proof is what separates a claim from a capability. This module produces the evidence that your leverage system delivers real results."

Three Types of Proof

  1. Before/After — Same task, done manually vs. done with your system. Side-by-side comparison.
  2. Time Proof — Measured time to complete a task with your system vs. estimated manual time.
  3. Quality Proof — Output quality comparison: is the AI-assisted output as good or better?

You need all three. Not one. All three.

Build Block (70 min)

Step 1: Before/After Comparison (25 min)

Select one task you've done manually before. Produce the output both ways:

  • Before: the manual output (use a past example from your actual work)
  • After: run the same task through your leverage system

Document side-by-side:

  • What does the manual version look like?
  • What does the system version look like?
  • What's the same? What's different? What's better? What's worse?

Step 2: Time Measurement (20 min)

Run your pipeline with a new input. Time it precisely:

  • Time from input to output (system): ___ minutes
  • Estimated time for manual equivalent: ___ hours
  • Leverage ratio: ___x

Be honest. If the system takes 20 minutes of human setup + 5 minutes of processing, the real time is 25 minutes, not 5.

Step 3: Quality Assessment (25 min)

Evaluate the system output against professional standards:

  • Would a client accept this as-is?
  • What human review/editing is needed?
  • Rate output quality: 1-10 compared to manual output
  • Identify quality gaps and note what would fix them

Review (10 min)

Each participant presents their proof:

  • Before/after side-by-side
  • Time comparison
  • Quality rating

Facilitator challenge: "Would a skeptical prospect believe this proof?"
